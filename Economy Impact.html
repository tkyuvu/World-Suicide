Besides social factors, economic factors have been suggested as a major influence on suicide. A slowdown in economy, job losses, business closures, increase in home bills, and more, negatively affect people's emotional health. GDP per capita, expenses, compensation, and unemployment rate are some factors that we can analyze to study . We are going to analyze the following economic factors to study if they have an impact on suicide rate:

  • GDP Per Capita

  • Expenses

  • Compensation

  • Unemployment Rate


    • Summary of each factor is listed below.

    GDP Per Capita

    Suicide rate decreased when the GDP per capita increased. There was a drop in GDP per capita from 2008 to 2009 because of the recession in 2008. Overall, the GDP per capita increased by year and there was a negative relationship with suicide rate.



    Expense (% GDP)

    The correlation between expense and suicide rate is positive. The higher the expense, the higher the suicide rate.

    Employee Compensation

    There is a negative relationship between the suicide rate and the employee compensation. Compensation for employee during their treatment and recovery from injuries at the work place has been shown that it has a great impact on employee emotional and psychological health. The suicide rate is higher in those who have lower or no compenstion.

    Unemployment Rate


    Both suicide rate and unemployment rate decreased from 1995 to 2008, suggesting a positive relationship between the two variables. When the unemployment rate is lower, suicide rate is also lower. After the recession in 2008, unemployment rate increased when suicide rate continued to drop. This is an evidence that there are many other reasons contributing to suicide. A single factor cannot be the sole reason that people commit suicide. Suicide happens because of multiple events in life.
