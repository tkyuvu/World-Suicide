There might be an association between the quality of life and suicide rate. Will healthcare have any effects on suicide? How about technology? Nowadays, interactions over the internet and cell phones also pose a risk of cyberbullying that some people have to face. We are going to explore the relationships between suicide rate and the following social factors:

  • Life Expectancy

  • Alcohol Consumption

  • Number of Physicians

  • Lower Education Completion

    Summary for each factor is listed below.

    Suicide rate vs Life Expectancy of 43 countries from 1995 to 2013

    This animated chart can help visualize the effect of life expectancy on suicide in addition to the line chart above.


    From the animated chart, the bubbles are moving towards the bottom right corner, meaning that suicide rate dropped as life expectancy increased. There was one country that behaved oddly when its life expectancy decreased from 1995 to 2005, then started to pick up in 2006. That country is South Africa. South Africa has a lower life expectancy than the rest of the world due to infectious diseases and violence. The reason for the drop in life expectancy of South Africa in 1995 - 2005 was HIV-AIDS epidemic. After HIV treatments became available in 2005, South Africa's life expectancy started to rise again.
    Except for South Africa, most countries' suicide rates dropped when life expectancy increased. We can say that there is a negative relationship between suicide rate and life expectancy.

    Number of Physicians

    The number of physicians increases by years, which means patients can have a better care in both mental and physical health. Therefore, suicide rate decreases when there are more healthcare workers.

    Alcohol Consumption

    The correlation between suicide rate and alcohol consumption is relatively strong. The positive trend is obviously seen in the chart. Studies have shown that alcohol is a major contributing factor in suicide. While it is impossible to narrow down the reasons people commit suicide, the abuse of alcohol is found to be related to nearly one-third of suicides. Alcohol is somewhat a "medication" people seek for when they suffer from anxiety. This substance may ease their mind at first, but the prolong use of alcohol can lead to strong suicidal thought. Additionally, alcohol generally makes the other contributing factors become worse. For example, alcohol can cause problems for someone who is dealing with depression and family/work issues, then they develope suicidal thought under alcohol influence.

    Technology (Internet and Cell Phone Usage)

    With the development in technology, more people (especially teenagers and young alduts) spend more time on the Internet and smart phones. Although the correlations in the charts above are weak, we can still see a positive trend for cell phone and internet usage. Heavy technology use and social media are key factors that change the habit of communication. There are plenty of information and influences on social media, both the good and the bad, but people's mental health are being affected by insulting comments or violent videos.

    Secondary Education Completion

    When looking at the trends from 1995 to 2013, there was a slight increase in secondary completion rate over the years and suicide rate decreased over years. This suggests that there is an association between education completion rate and suicide rate. Higher education completion is associated with lower suicide rate.
